# GPT-3 can transform cloze deletion prompts into question-answer prompts

[[Cloze deletion]]-based prompts are typically much easier and faster to write than full question/answer prompts. But [[Cloze deletion prompts seem to produce less understanding than question-answer pairs in spaced repetition memory systems]]. One interesting “middle path” may be to use language models to generate question/answer prompts from cloze deletions. Initial experiments with [[Ozzie Kirkby]] ([[2021-06-10]]) showed surprising promise.

Trying again with [[GPT-4]] on [[2023-06-14]], I had immediate success with this prompt: [[20230614095308]]. Also worked pretty well with GPT-3.5-turbo, though results were slightly less good.

See also: [[Cloze deletions can be used to create typical two-sided flashcards]].
