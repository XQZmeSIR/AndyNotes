# Mnemonic medium

The mnemonic medium embeds a [[Spaced repetition memory system]] within narrative prose. [[Spaced repetition memory systems make memory a choice]], but they’ve suffered significant barriers to adoption. The mnemonic medium is designed to solve many of these problems.

It was first used in [[Quantum Country]]. I’m now working to expand its scope considerably in [[Orbit]]. (see more [[Substantive examples of the mnemonic medium]])

  * Key design properties:
    * [[The mnemonic medium gives structure to normally-atomized spaced repetition memory prompts]]
    * [[The mnemonic medium supplies expert-authored prompts to remove the burden of prompt-writing]]
    * [[Mnemonic medium prompts are interleaved into the reading experience]]
    * [[The mnemonic medium may help scaffold prompt-writing through author-provided prompts]]
    * [[The mnemonic medium’s design relies on light-weight prompts]]
    * [[The critical thing to optimize in spaced repetition memory systems is emotional connection to the review session and its contents]]

  * Challenges
    * [[The early mnemonic medium’s structures are too rigid for others to help define the medium]]
    * … to adoption by end-users:
    * [[Many people view memory as unimportant to deep creative work]]
    * [[The initial mnemonic medium is implicitly authoritarian in premise]]
      * [[The mnemonic medium should give readers control over the prompts they collect]]
    * [[Mnemonic medium onboarding depends on a critical mass of content]]
      * [[Spaced repetition review sessions often become boring and detached without a steady stream of new prompts]]
    * [[Mass adoption of the mnemonic medium seems to require mass adoption of web publishing]]
      * [[Reading texts on computers is unpleasant]]
    * [[Regular spaced repetition memory practice is an onerous habit to adopt]]
      * [[Spaced repetition memory systems don’t rapidly demonstrate their benefits]]
      * [[Spaced repetition memory systems make you feel like your memory is worse than it really is]]
    * … to efficacy:
    * [[Retrieval practice and transfer learning]] (I don’t think the MM performs very well here)
    * [[Active learning insights need memory support too]]
    * … for designers:
    * [[The dominant culture around spaced repetition memory systems is fixated on meaningless goals]]
    * [[The mnemonic medium should be developed in a context where people really need fluency]]
    * [[I can’t easily iterate on the design of the mnemonic medium by testing it on myself]]
    * [[Quantum Country lacks a coherent spatial representation of the mnemonic medium’s cards as objects]]
    * [[Extremely data-dense texts need a different mnemonic medium design]]
    * [[Aggressively scaling the mnemonic medium in 2020 is premature]]
    * … for authors
    * [[Writing good prompts for others is likely harder than writing good prompts for oneself]]
    * [[How often and where should mnemonic medium review areas be inserted?]]
