# The Primer doesn’t appear to coerce its students

One reason that people find the Primer so inspiring is that it paints a vision of an educational experience which succeeds without obviously coercing or confining its students, as ordinary schools so stereotypically do.

This is mostly an outright fantasy: [[The Primer’s activities are only intrinsically meaningful to Nell because her life is impoverished]]. Somewhat more deeply, it’s also an illusion because while the Primer doesn’t _outwardly_ coerce Nell, it does presume to manipulate her with various psychological tricks in pursuit of its specific goals, while [[Nell doesn’t know or share the Primer’s goals]].
