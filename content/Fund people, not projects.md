# Fund people, not projects

This Alan Kay coinage summarizes the stance that [[Talented people are the high-order bit]], so if you’re a hiring manager or a funder, you should think primarily about choosing the _people_ you want to hire/fund—and don’t worry so much about the specifics of a given project.

It’s not clear that this is always the best strategy. Probably it varies by research area and various other factors. See Artir’s [[meta- review]] of the efficacy question.

Related: [[Talented researchers should be funded, not managed]].
