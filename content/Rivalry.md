# Rivalry

A resource is **rivalrous** if one person’s consumption reduces another person’s ability to consume (e.g. foods, most private goods) and **non- rivalrous** if the marginal cost of providing the resource to an extra person is zero (e.g. knowledge, most software, a scenic view).

[[Elinor Ostrom]] suggests that we should think about this as a continuous metric of _subtractability,_ rather than a binary attribute.

Q. What’s a (new) example of a rivalrous resource? A. (e.g. a computer, a watermelon, a fishery, a river’s outflow)

Q. What’s a (new) example of a non-rivalrous resource? A. (e.g. knowledge, a scenic view, broadcast television)
