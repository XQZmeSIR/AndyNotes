# Implicit retrieval practice

[[Retrieval practice]] which occurs implicitly in the course of other, possibly more authentic activities. Unclear whether and in what scenarios this has the same effect as explicit retrieval practice.

  * [[Tekin, E., & Roediger, H. L. (2021). The effect of delayed judgments of learning on retention. Metacognition and Learning.]] find that [[Judgment of learning]] produces a smaller effect on memory than explicit retrieval practice. This could be evidence either that [[Judgment of learning]] doesn’t involve covert retrieval, or that covert retrieval has a smaller effect than overt retrieval.
